LECTURE 6 AND 7 A MAN IN THE MIDDLE ATTACT

A cyberattack known as a man-in-the-middle (MiTM) attack involves the perpetrator discreetly intercepting and relaying messages between two parties who believe they are speaking directly to one another. The attack is a form of eavesdropping in which the assailant overhears the full discussion before taking control of it. Because they allow the attacker to obtain and manipulate sensitive personal information like login credentials, account information, or credit card numbers in real time, MiTM cyber assaults pose a major risk to online security. The following steps are involved in a common data interception technique: An attacker installs a packet sniffer to gauge any network traffic that might be insecure, such as a user accessing a Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP)-based website or using a non-secure public hotspot. Once the user logs into the insecure website, the attacker retrieves the user's information and redirects them to a fake website. The fake website mimics the original website and gathers all the pertinent user data, which the attacker can then use to access all the user resources on the original website.
